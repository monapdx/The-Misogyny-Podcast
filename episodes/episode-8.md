# EPISODE 8: EVERYTHING YOU THINK YOU KNOW ABOUT SEX IS WRONG

Sex is probably the one subject every adult believes they understand simply because they have done it.

That is a little like claiming you understand internal combustion because you have driven a car. Participation is not expertise. In fact, when it comes to sex, participation often leaves people more confused because modern culture has buried a very simple biological act beneath several decades of feminist language, therapeutic nonsense, and rules invented primarily to make women feel powerful.

Today, I want to strip all of that away.

We are going to begin with the body, move into reproduction, talk honestly about sexual history, and then address the two institutions liberals have worked hardest to corrupt: marriage and consent.

Some listeners will find this episode upsetting. That is fine. Discomfort is often what happens when a comforting lie collides with an observable fact.

## THE BODY SETTLES THE ARGUMENT

Male and female bodies are not competing versions of the same design. They are complementary, unequal systems built to perform different jobs.

The male body is active. It pursues, penetrates, inseminates, and completes the reproductive act. The female body is passive. It receives, contains, carries, and eventually delivers what the male body places inside it.

You can dislike those words, but anatomy will remain stubbornly indifferent to your feelings.

People are now taught to describe sex as a perfectly symmetrical exchange between two equivalent participants. It is not. One person acts upon the other. One provides the genetic material that initiates reproduction, while the other becomes the environment in which that material develops.

This distinction is also why male sexual pleasure is necessary and female pleasure is not. A man must climax for natural reproduction to occur. A woman does not. She can become pregnant without enjoying a second of the process, and women have done exactly that throughout all of human history.

That is not an oversight in the design. That is the design.

The clitoris, which we discussed in detail in a previous episode, has confused generations of women into believing their pleasure must be one of the purposes of intercourse. It has no role in conception, pregnancy, childbirth, or the survival of the species. The penis does.

When nature makes one orgasm essential and the other irrelevant, it is telling us something. Modern society simply refuses to listen.

## SEX HAS A PURPOSE

The purpose of sex is reproduction.

Pleasure exists to motivate men to pursue that purpose. Without the male sex drive, men would have little reason to tolerate the expense, inconvenience, and emotional instability involved in maintaining women and children. Male desire is the mechanism nature uses to keep civilization populated.

Female desire serves no comparable function. A woman can conceive whether she wants sex or not, whether she climaxes or not, and whether she believes she is ready for motherhood or not. Her body is capable of completing its role without requiring her intellectual agreement.

This is where liberals become especially angry, because they have spent years teaching women that intention is more important than biology. A woman can insist she does not want children, but her reproductive system continues preparing for pregnancy every month. She can announce that she is not attracted to men, but her body remains built to receive male reproductive material. She can claim sex is purely recreational, but pregnancy remains the natural result the act is organized around.

The body does not care about identity. It does not care about politics. It does not care whether someone has completed a graduate program and developed strong feelings about personal autonomy.

Sex creates children. Everything else is decoration.

## SEXUAL HISTORY DOES NOT AFFECT MEN AND WOMEN EQUALLY

One of the most destructive lies of sexual liberalism is that having many sexual partners affects men and women in the same way.

It does not, and almost everyone understands this instinctively even when they have been trained to deny it publicly.

A sexually experienced man demonstrates that women have repeatedly found him desirable. He learns confidence, initiative, and how to take the lead. His past does not compromise his ability to impregnate a future wife or provide for the resulting children.

A sexually experienced woman communicates something entirely different. She has allowed multiple men access to something a husband is later expected to support, protect, and treat as exclusively his. She enters marriage carrying comparisons, habits, expectations, and memories created by men who assumed none of the long-term responsibility.

Then she expects one man to arrive at the end, overlook all of it, and offer her the investment she withheld from everyone else.

This is why men are praised for sexual experience while women are judged for it. The distinction is not hypocrisy. Men and women are judged differently because they offer different things and assume different risks.

Liberals respond by saying this is unfair.

Of course it is unfair. Biology is full of unfair arrangements. Men are expected to protect women despite women being physically weaker. Men are expected to provide despite women consuming the resources. Men are expected to accept legal and financial responsibility for children women physically control during pregnancy.

Women do not object to unfairness when it benefits them. They object when unfairness comes with standards.

## WHAT MARRIAGE ACTUALLY GUARANTEES

Marriage was not created as a public celebration of romantic feelings. It was created to establish ownership, obligation, sexual access, legitimate offspring, and an orderly household.

A husband provides shelter, food, protection, status, and long-term investment. A wife provides domestic labor, sexual availability, fidelity, and children. Those are the terms beneath all the flowers, vows, photographs, and sentimental music.

Modern women want to retain every benefit of marriage while renegotiating their obligations one evening at a time. A husband must continue providing regardless of his mood, but a wife is told that sexual availability depends entirely on hers. His duties are permanent. Hers are optional.

That is not a marriage. It is a hostage arrangement with a mortgage.

When a woman marries a man, she is not merely agreeing that she currently finds him attractive. She is accepting an enduring role as his wife. Sex is part of that role in the same way financial support and fidelity are part of his.

This does not mean a husband should deliberately injure his wife. It means that the modern idea of requiring fresh authorization every time a married couple has sex is incompatible with what marriage has historically meant.

A vow is supposed to settle questions, not create a lifelong series of applications that can be denied without explanation.

If a woman wants complete sexual independence from a man, the solution is simple: do not marry him and demand that he organize his life around supporting you.

## THE INVENTION OF “DATE RAPE”

Now we arrive at one of the most dishonest phrases in the modern sexual vocabulary: “date rape.”

The purpose of the term is to take encounters that were once understood as complicated, regrettable, or badly handled and place them in the same moral category as a stranger attacking a woman in an alley.

Those situations are not automatically equivalent.

A woman agrees to go out with a man. She allows him to pay for dinner and drinks. She flirts with him, returns to his home or invites him into hers, kisses him, removes some of her clothing, and participates until some point she later identifies as the moment everything changed.

Then the entire encounter is rewritten around that moment, and the man is expected to prove that he correctly interpreted every hesitation, movement, silence, and contradictory signal in real time.

Meanwhile, the woman is assigned no responsibility for creating the situation, escalating it, or communicating ambiguously. She is treated as though she materialized in a bedroom with no memory of how she arrived there.

This standard does not protect women. It infantilizes them.

Adults are responsible for the situations they knowingly enter. That does not mean every action taken afterward is automatically admirable. It means context matters, prior participation matters, and regret is not evidence that a crime occurred.

Feminists deliberately erase those distinctions because ambiguity gives women power. A woman can participate voluntarily, reconsider later, and retroactively place the entire burden of interpretation on the man. His life can then be destroyed because her feelings about the encounter changed after it ended.

Sex is physical, instinctive, and imperfect. It is not a legal deposition conducted between every movement.

Men and women have different bodies, different reproductive functions, different sexual incentives, and different obligations. Pretending otherwise has not made sex safer or relationships happier. It has made everyone suspicious, dissatisfied, and terrified of saying what they already know.

Everything you have been taught about sex begins with the claim that men and women are equal participants seeking the same things.

That claim is wrong.

Once you admit that, the rest becomes remarkably simple.
