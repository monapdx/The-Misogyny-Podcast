Episode 12: Dr. Alters of Weird Science Surgical Group — Part One

Derek: Greetings listeners, it's Derek Vale, your host of the Misogyny Podcast.

Today we have a great interview scheduled for you. We'll be talking with Dr. Alters of the Weird Science Surgical Group, his plastic surgery practice that's been offering women their services for nearly twenty-five years.

Derek: Welcome to the show, doctor.

Dr. Alters: Thank you for having me, Derek!

Derek: Absolutely! We're excited to talk with you about the ways you have helped married couples keep their relationship healthy and exciting for a lot longer than they probably could on their own.

Dr. Alters laughs

Dr. Alters: Well, Derek, you know, that's what I'm all about. No husband wants to stay with a woman who lets herself go a few years into the relationship, right?

Derek: You know, I wish more women today understood this. It seems like the more they spout off about feminism, the uglier they think its okay to let themselves become. I really don't get it.

Dr. Alters: Hmmm. Yeah, you know, Derek, I don't understand it either to be honest.

Derek: Well, let's start this off by letting you tell our listeners a little bit about your practice, the story behind it, and what you and your company have to offer married couples.

Dr. Alters: Sure! I'd be happy to! So the Weird Science Surgical Group is a family business. My father was a plastic surgeon before me and he founded the practice many years ago now. Of course, he's retired these days, but I do my best to carry on the same spirit that he started it with and do my best to keep the mission focused the way he always managed to.

Beyond family ties, the real reason I chose to lead my father's practice when he retired is actually pretty personal. I was almost married myself years ago, to a woman we will just call Linda. I was ready to get married after about a year of dating. Everything had been going remarkably well and I liked her quite a bit.

Derek: Uh-oh. I'm waiting for the BUT...

Dr. Alters: Ha, yes...well, you know, she was almost perfect. But I'd been clear with her from the outset of things that she needed a couple of refinements. Okay, I'll be honest. She needed a couple refinements in addition to at least one more invasive alteration.

I would have been happy to do it all for her too!

Derek: Do you mind my asking what she needed done, doctor?

Dr. Alters: sighs Well...her nose, for one. It needed a total overhaul. That was the most glaring thing, for me at least. She had respectable breasts, but I would have preferred at least a cup size more if she'd just been willing to go under my knife a few times.

If she'd let me take care of those two areas, we'd probably still be together.

Derek: I wonder what gave her the idea that refusing was acceptable after a year of dating a man.

Dr. Alters: I was also pretty confused about that. I mean, I was ready to propose and make her my wife as soon as the surgeries were complete.

Derek: Well that was obviously her loss in the greater scheme of things, but why was she so unwilling?

Dr. Alters: For reasons I'll never understand, she didn't believe in changing one's natural appearance. I'm pretty sure after I moved on, she joined some strange commune where they didn't allow women to wear makeup and she became some strange vegetarian who got all political or something. At least, that's what I heard through the grapevine at that time.

shrugs

Derek: You dodged a bullet there, doctor. A vegetarian and political? It sounds like the nose was only the beginning.

Dr. Alters: Exactly. And that experience taught me something important: physical flaws can be corrected, but only if the patient possesses the emotional maturity to accept that someone else knows what is best for her.

Derek: That’s profound.

Dr. Alters: Thank you. It became the guiding principle behind the Weird Science Surgical Group as it exists today. We don’t treat cosmetic surgery as some frivolous act of personal expression. We treat it as relationship maintenance.

Derek: So when a woman comes into your office, you’re looking beyond what she wants?

Dr. Alters: Much further beyond it. Women are often too close to their own bodies to evaluate them objectively. They’ve spent their entire lives looking at the same face in the mirror. Naturally, they become sentimental about it.

Derek: Familiarity mistaken for quality.

Dr. Alters: Precisely. That’s why every initial consultation begins with what we call the Domestic Aesthetic Assessment. We ask about her age, marital status, weight history, reproductive plans, and the length of her current relationship. Then we ask the most important question.

Derek: Which is?

Dr. Alters: “What does your husband think?”

Derek: Finally, a medical professional asking for the relevant opinion.

Dr. Alters: You’d be surprised how often a patient says, “I’m doing this for myself.”

Derek: I’m already suspicious.

Dr. Alters: As you should be. “For myself” is usually marketing language she picked up from some lifestyle magazine. If the procedure isn’t improving her value within the relationship, then what exactly are we accomplishing?

Derek: So do you prefer that husbands attend the consultation?

Dr. Alters: We strongly encourage it. In fact, we offer a complimentary private session with the husband before speaking to the patient.

Derek: Without her in the room?

Dr. Alters: Of course. A man can’t be expected to discuss his wife’s shortcomings honestly while she’s sitting three feet away becoming emotional.

Derek: That’s just good clinical practice.

Dr. Alters: He can bring photographs, measurements, even examples of women whose appearance he considers more successful. Then my staff and I build a realistic treatment plan.

Derek: “More successful.” I like that.

Dr. Alters: Beauty is an outcome, Derek. People dislike admitting it because outcomes can be measured, and anything measurable creates the possibility that someone might fall below average.

Derek: God forbid anyone receive information they didn’t specifically authorize.

Dr. Alters: Exactly. Once we’ve completed the husband’s consultation, we invite his wife in and present our recommendations.

Derek: How do women usually react?

Dr. Alters: There are generally three stages. First, surprise. Then defensiveness. Finally, gratitude.

Derek: How long does it take to reach gratitude?

Dr. Alters: Sometimes several years.

Derek: laughs But they get there eventually.

Dr. Alters: Or they stop returning our calls. Either way, the treatment team has done its job.

Derek: What are the procedures you recommend most often?

Dr. Alters: Breast augmentation remains popular, naturally. Rhinoplasty is another staple. We also perform liposuction, tummy tucks, facial rejuvenation, and what we call the Anniversary Restoration Package.

Derek: That sounds promising. What does it include?

Dr. Alters: It depends on the marriage. We usually recommend it around the ten-year anniversary, although some women require intervention much earlier. The package combines several procedures into one coordinated plan intended to restore the appearance the husband reasonably believed he was marrying.

Derek: Almost like returning a house to its condition when the lease was signed.

Dr. Alters: I generally avoid comparing women to real estate.

Derek: Right. A house appreciates.

Dr. Alters laughs

Dr. Alters: You said it, not me.

Derek: What role does pregnancy play in all this? Because women love mentioning childbirth whenever anyone notices that their bodies have changed.

Dr. Alters: Pregnancy certainly creates challenges. But having fulfilled one obligation doesn’t permanently excuse a woman from the others. We offer a postpartum program designed to return patients to a presentable condition as efficiently as possible.

Derek: How soon after delivery can a husband contact you?

Dr. Alters: Immediately. Ideally before delivery, actually. Planning ahead prevents an awkward conversation later.

Derek: That’s smart. Make the appointment while everyone is still pretending the baby is the only major change coming to the household.

Dr. Alters: Precisely. We can’t always operate right away, but we can assess the situation, establish expectations, and reserve a place on the schedule.

Derek: Do you ever encounter husbands whose expectations are unrealistic?

Dr. Alters: Certainly. Some men bring in photographs that are simply incompatible with their wives’ underlying anatomy.

Derek: So you tell them no?

Dr. Alters: We tell them it may require multiple procedures.

Derek: That’s the entrepreneurial spirit.

Dr. Alters: Medicine is about finding solutions.

Derek: Now, I want to address something that I’m sure our more fragile listeners are already screaming at their screens. What about consent?

Dr. Alters: Every patient signs the appropriate forms.

Derek: There you have it.

Dr. Alters: In all seriousness, Derek, we never operate without formal consent. But I reject the idea that consent must be free from persuasion. Doctors persuade patients to stop smoking. Families persuade elderly relatives to surrender their car keys. Why should a husband be forbidden from persuading his wife to correct a problem affecting them both?

Derek: Especially when he’s the one expected to look at the result every day.

Dr. Alters: Exactly. Her body may belong to her in the narrow legal sense, but its condition has consequences for the entire marriage.

Derek: I imagine critics accuse your practice of reinforcing unrealistic beauty standards.

Dr. Alters: Constantly. My response is always the same: if the standards were truly unrealistic, my patients wouldn’t leave the operating room meeting them.

Derek: That is devastatingly simple.

Dr. Alters: People confuse “difficult” with “impossible.” Maintaining a marriage is difficult. Raising children is difficult. Recovering from six elective procedures performed during the same week is difficult. Adults do difficult things.

Derek: Usually women complain that men should simply love them as they are.

Dr. Alters: And I’m sure men would love to remain exactly as they are too—unemployed, unwashed, and living in someone’s basement. Yet women insist they improve themselves.

Derek: They’re very demanding that way.

Dr. Alters: Relationships involve expectations. The difference is that men are shamed for expressing theirs clearly.

Derek: Doctor, before we take a short break, tell listeners where they can learn more about your practice.

Dr. Alters: They can visit the Weird Science Surgical Group website and complete our confidential Marriage Viability Questionnaire. It takes about ten minutes, although the husband’s portion may take longer depending on the number of concerns he needs to document.

Derek: And you offer financing?

Dr. Alters: Absolutely. A woman’s appearance should never deteriorate merely because her husband lacks immediate liquidity.

Derek: Beautifully put. Stay with us. When we return, Dr. Alters will tell us about the strangest requests he’s received, the procedures women most commonly regret refusing, and why he believes “aging gracefully” is just surrender with better public relations.
